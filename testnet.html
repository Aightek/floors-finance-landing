What defines a floor price?

Floors Finance uses Protocol Owned Liquidity to back tokens. This creates a programmatic guarantee where the treasury acts as the redeemer at the floor price — backed by real reserves, not promises.

Is there liquidation risk?

Zero. Because you borrow against the floor price, and the floor is backed by hard reserves, your collateral value can never drop below your debt position. No margin calls. No health factors.

How does the floor price rise?

Trading fees, liquidity rebalancing, and staking yield all compound into the protocol's reserves. As reserves grow relative to supply, the floor rises automatically — no action needed from holders.

What are Genesis Rewards?

Points earned during the Genesis Testnet directly determine your share of the token genesis distribution. High earners secure priority access to vault capacity and the first tier of protocol voting power.

Has the protocol been audited?

Yes. The Floors Finance protocol has been independently audited. The full report is available under Protocol → Audit in the footer. Security is a first-class concern at every layer of the system.

When does Phase One end?

Phase One is live now. Once the presale closes, the token floor is set permanently. Every participant after the close only pushes it higher — join early for the lowest floor ever.

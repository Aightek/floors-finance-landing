What defines a floor price?

Floors Finance uses Protocol Owned Liquidity (POL) to back tokens. This creates a programmatic guarantee where the treasury acts as the redeemer at the floor price — backed by real reserves, not promises.

Is there liquidation risk?

Zero. Because you borrow against the floor price, and the floor price is backed by hard reserves, your collateral value can never drop below your debt position. No margin calls. No health factors.

What are the tier thresholds?

T1 requires 5,000+ points and unlocks maximum allocation multipliers and first vault access. T2 starts at 2,000 points. T3 at 500. Incomplete (INC) wallets still earn a base allocation — but T1 earns disproportionately more.

How do points convert to tokens?

Your final points balance at Phase One close determines your proportional share of the genesis allocation pool — 15% of total Floors Token supply. Higher points = larger share. Tier bonuses apply on top of your base allocation.

Has the protocol been audited?

Yes. The Floors Finance protocol has been independently audited. The full report is available under Protocol → Audit in the footer. Security is a first-class concern at every layer of the system.

When does Phase One end?

Phase One is live now. Once the presale closes, the token floor is set permanently. Every participant after the close only pushes it higher — join early for the lowest floor ever.
